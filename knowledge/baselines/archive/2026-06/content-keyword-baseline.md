Draft FAQ Suggestions

Troubleshooting FAQ Drafts

Draft FAQ: What kinds of problems require electrical troubleshooting?

Draft answer: Common examples include flickering lights, outlets that stop working, switches that act strangely, partial power loss, buzzing, and breakers that trip more than they should.

Draft FAQ: Can you tell what is wrong before visiting?

Draft answer: Some issues have common patterns, but accurate troubleshooting usually requires checking the affected circuits, devices, and panel on-site.

Panel / Upgrade FAQ Drafts

Draft FAQ: What is the difference between panel replacement and a service upgrade?

Draft answer: Panel replacement usually focuses on changing the panel itself. A service upgrade usually means increasing the electrical capacity feeding the home. Some projects involve one, and some involve both.

Draft FAQ: How do I know if my home may need an upgrade?

Draft answer: Common reasons include remodel plans, added electrical load, EV charger installation, or a panel that no longer fits the needs of the house.

EV Charger FAQ Drafts

Draft FAQ: Do all homes need a panel upgrade for an EV charger?

Draft answer: Not always. It depends on the existing electrical system, the charger type, and the overall load in the home.

Draft FAQ: Can you help decide where the charger should go?

Draft answer: Yes, charger location planning is part of making sure the setup is practical, clean, and convenient to use.
